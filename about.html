Lucas Reed is an engineer, musician, and writer from Highland, California, where the San Bernardino Mountains hold their dark shape against the sky and the wind through San Gorgonio Pass sounds like something trying to speak.

By day he builds infrastructure for the freight and passenger rail systems that move the West Coast. By night he writes about the distances between worlds, the ones we can measure and the ones we can't.

Toward the Shape of Time is his first novel. It began as a question he couldn't stop asking: what would a father leave behind if he found a door to somewhere impossible, and what would his son do to follow him through it?

He lives with his wife Amber, their daughters Margot and Sloane, and two Labradoodles who have never once respected a boundary of any kind.
