The Science

How the universe works. As far as anyone can tell.

Time

Time only moves forward. There are no exceptions. No loopholes. No clever tricks that let you go back and fix something. The arrow holds.

But time doesn't move at the same speed everywhere.

Your body ages at the rate of your origin world. If you were born on Earth, your cells keep Earth's clock no matter where you go. But your experience, your perception, runs at local speed. On Vaelith, you live a full day while Earth barely notices you're gone.

This isn't relativity. It's something else. Something the tunnels do.

The Tunnels

FTL conduits that thread through gravitational curvature. They connect specific points across space, planet to planet, and the path between them determines the dilation ratio.

Earth to Vaelith: 365 to 1. One Earth day equals one Vaelith year.

The tunnels are old. Older than anyone who uses them. They weren't built, at least not in any way that implies a builder with hands. They were grown. Cultivated. Seeded into the fabric of spacetime by something patient enough to wait for them to mature.

Not all tunnels are open. Not all of them are safe.

The Boundaries

Wounds in spacetime. Places where the membrane between worlds is thin enough to pass through, if you have the right resonance.

They breathe. Open and shut on rhythms no one has fully mapped. Most are one-way by default. You go through. You don't come back the same way.

The boundaries aren't dangerous in the way fire is dangerous. They're dangerous in the way a decision is dangerous. Once you cross, you've made a choice that reshapes every choice that follows.

The Artifact

A fragment of the Tessara. A seed left behind, or sent ahead, depending on your perspective.

It stabilizes crossings. It resonates with compatible minds. It stores data in ways no human instrument can read, and it acts with what some might call intention.

It is not a tool. Tools don't choose their users.
